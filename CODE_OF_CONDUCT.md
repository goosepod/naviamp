# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Naviamp to be a respectful and welcoming project for everyone who chooses to participate.

Contributors, maintainers, and users are expected to interact in a way that is professional, constructive, and considerate of differing viewpoints and experience levels.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that helps the project:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of different opinions and experiences.
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback.
- Staying focused on what is best for the project and its users.
- Showing patience with people who are learning or asking questions in good faith.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
- Insulting or derogatory comments.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing someone else's private information without permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery where it is not appropriate.
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or bad-faith participation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently limit participation for behavior they determine to be harmful, inappropriate, threatening, or disruptive.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainer through GitHub. If the issue is sensitive, contact the maintainer privately rather than opening a public issue.

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as possible. The maintainer will respect the privacy and safety of the reporter when handling the issue.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other community spaces connected to Naviamp.

It also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces.
